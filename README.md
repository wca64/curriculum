# ♙ **LEVEL 01 – FOUNDATION**

| # | Lesson Name | What You’ll Learn |
|:-:|---|---|
| 01 | [Introduction to Chess](LEVEL%2001%20-%20FOUNDATION/01%20-%20Introduction%20to%20Chess) | • To understand what chess is and how it's played <br> • To explore the origin and history of chess <br> • To learn about famous chess champions (men & women) <br> • To understand how chess helps the brain and why it's fun to learn |
| 02 | [The Chessboard](LEVEL%2001%20-%20FOUNDATION/02%20-%20The%20Chessboard) | • How to recognize the layout and colors of the chessboard <br> • How to name squares using files and ranks <br> • How to place the board correctly <br> • How to set up all chess pieces in their starting positions <br> • Understanding which side moves first and why |
| 03 | [The Pieces (Part 1)](LEVEL%2001%20-%20FOUNDATION/03%20-%20The%20Pieces%20(Part%201)) | • How pieces move: Rook, Bishop, Queen, Knight and King <br> • Attacking and Capturing ( RBQNK ) <br> • Understanding the value of each piece |
| 04 | [The Pieces (Part 2)](LEVEL%2001%20-%20FOUNDATION/04%20-%20The%20Pieces%20(Part%202)) | • Pawn moves <br> • Attacking and Capturing with pawn <br> • Pawn Promotion |
| 05 | [Protecting Your Pieces](LEVEL%2001%20-%20FOUNDATION/05%20-%20Protecting%20Your%20Pieces) | • How to protect your pieces when they are in danger <br> • Four main ways to defend in chess <br> • When to move, protect, block, or capture during an attack <br> • How to make smart and safe defensive decisions instead of panic moves |
| 06 | [Check & Escaping Check](LEVEL%2001%20-%20FOUNDATION/06%20-%20Check%20%26%20Escaping%20Check) | • What a “check” is and why it is the most urgent threat in chess <br> • What to do when your king is in check — the 3 legal ways to escape <br> • When to move, capture, or block to stay safe <br> • Why the king can never be captured and why two kings cannot stand next to each other |
| 07 | [Understanding Checkmate](LEVEL%2001%20-%20FOUNDATION/07%20-%20Understanding%20Checkmate) | • What checkmate means and how it ends the game <br> • How to recognize a checkmate position <br> • The difference between check and checkmate <br> • How teamwork between pieces creates a successful mate <br> • Why stopping checkmate is impossible once all escape squares are covered |
| 08 | [Mate in One](LEVEL%2001%20-%20FOUNDATION/08%20-%20Mate%20in%20One) | • How to deliver checkmate in exactly one move <br> • How to spot forced mate patterns quickly <br> • How to visualize escape squares and eliminate them instantly <br> • How to use a single piece to finish the game when the opportunity appears |
| 09 | [Special Moves](LEVEL%2001%20-%20FOUNDATION/09%20-%20Special%20Moves) | • How to castle safely and when castling is allowed or forbidden <br> • How to perform kingside and queenside castling correctly <br> • Why castling protects the king and activates the rook <br> • How the special pawn rule en passant works <br> • When en passant is possible — and when it is not |
| 10 | [Smart Exchanges](LEVEL%2001%20-%20FOUNDATION/10%20-%20Smart%20Exchanges) | • How trading pieces works in chess <br> • The standard value of each chess piece <br> • How to judge whether an exchange is good, equal, or bad <br> • How to make trades that lead to material advantage and avoid traps |
| 11 | [Double Pressure](LEVEL%2001%20-%20FOUNDATION/11%20-%20Double%20Pressure) | • How to attack the same piece or target twice at the same time <br> • How to win material using double pressure <br> • How to calculate captures in the correct order <br> • How attackers and defenders decide who wins the fight over a piece |
| 12 | [Saving the Draw](LEVEL%2001%20-%20FOUNDATION/12%20-%20Saving%20the%20Draw) | • What a draw means in chess <br> • How a stalemate happens <br> • Situations where neither player can win <br> • Why a winning position can sometimes turn into a draw |
| 13 | [Chess Move Notation](LEVEL%2001%20-%20FOUNDATION/13%20-%20Chess%20Move%20Notation) | • How to write and read chess moves using short algebraic notation <br> • How to record captures, checks, checkmates, castling, and pawn promotion <br> • How to fill a scoresheet and understand game results <br> • How to play through a recorded game move-by-move |
