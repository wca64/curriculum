# ♘ **LEVEL 02 – BEGINNER**

| # | Lesson Name | What You’ll Learn |
|:-:|---|---|
| 01 | [Piece Activity](LEVEL%2002%20-%20BEGINNER/01%20-%20Piece%20Activity) | • How to make your pieces stronger by placing them on active squares <br> • Why controlling the center increases power, mobility, and attacking chances <br> • How to identify passive pieces and improve their placement <br> • How to trap or restrict your opponent’s pieces using activity <br> • How piece coordination and mobility decide who wins the battle |
| 02 | [Attack Targets](LEVEL%2002%20-%20BEGINNER/02%20-%20Attack%20Targets) | • The 3 main targets you can attack in chess <br> • How to identify weak or valuable targets quickly <br> • When and how to apply pressure on pieces, squares, and the king <br> • How to use your pieces to create threats and gain advantage |
| 03 | [Double Attack: Queen](LEVEL%2002%20-%20BEGINNER/03%20-%20Double%20Attack%3A%20Queen) | • How the queen can attack two targets at the same time <br> • How to identify the right moment and the right square for a queen double attack <br> • How to use the queen to win material or deliver checkmate <br> • How to avoid mistakes and stay safe while attempting a double attack |
| 04 | [The Pin](LEVEL%2002%20-%20BEGINNER/04%20-%20The%20Pin) | • What a pin is and how it restricts a piece <br> • The different types of pins and how they change a position <br> • How to identify, set up, and exploit pins to win material <br> • How to recognize when a pinned piece cannot move — and when it actually can <br> • How to use ranks, files, and diagonals to lock pieces in place |
| 05 | [Basic Checkmates](LEVEL%2002%20-%20BEGINNER/05%20-%20Basic%20Checkmates) | • checkmating the lone king: using 2 rooks (ladder mate) <br> • checkmating the lone king: using a queen |
| 06 | [Removing the Defender: Win Material](LEVEL%2002%20-%20BEGINNER/06%20-%20Removing%20the%20Defender%3A%20Win%20Material) | • What a defender is and why it matters in tactics <br> • How to remove, chase, or lure away a defender to win material <br> • How to identify targets protected only once and turn that into an advantage <br> • When removing a defender is safe, profitable, or dangerous <br> • How to spot forced wins created by weakening the defense |
| 07 | [Opening Golden Rules](LEVEL%2002%20-%20BEGINNER/07%20-%20Opening%20Golden%20Rules) | • How to begin a chess game with strong and safe opening moves <br> • How to develop pieces efficiently without wasting time <br> • Why controlling the center helps every part of your strategy <br> • How to keep your king safe and avoid early blunders |
| 08 | [Mate in Two](LEVEL%2002%20-%20BEGINNER/08%20-%20Mate%20in%20Two) | • How to deliver checkmate in exactly two moves <br> • How to visualize ahead and plan short combinations <br> • How to build a checkmate pattern using two pieces <br> • How to make pieces work together to trap the king |
| 09 | [Double Attack: Knight Forks](LEVEL%2002%20-%20BEGINNER/09%20-%20Double%20Attack%3A%20Knight%20Forks) | • How knights deliver double attacks — also called forks <br> • How to find knight jumps that attack two targets at the same time <br> • How to spot hanging or weakly defended pieces as fork targets <br> • When a knight fork wins material or threatens checkmate <br> • How knight forks differ from queen double attacks, and when knights are stronger |
| 10 | [Rook Mate](LEVEL%2002%20-%20BEGINNER/10%20-%20Rook%20Mate) | • How to checkmate a lone king using your king and rook together <br> • A clear 3-step plan: coordinate, push to the edge, deliver mate <br> • Why the rook cannot mate alone and needs help from its king <br> • How to use “waiting moves” to trap the enemy king in the corner <br> • How to avoid stalemate and simple blunders while doing rook mate |
| 11 | [Double Attack: RBPK](LEVEL%2002%20-%20BEGINNER/11%20-%20Double%20Attack%3A%20RBPK) | • How to create double attacks using rook, bishop, pawn and king <br> • How to recognize target combinations and pick the right piece for each situation <br> • How to use two pieces working together to build a stronger double attack <br> • A simple search strategy for finding and setting up double attacks in real games |
| 12 | [Discovered Attack](LEVEL%2002%20-%20BEGINNER/12%20-%20Discovered%20Attack) | • How discovered attacks work as a powerful indirect double attack <br> • The roles of the front piece and back piece in a battery <br> • How to use discovered check and discovered attacks to win material or give mate <br> • When a sacrifice of the front piece is correct to open the line <br> • How to spot, set up, and defend against discovered attacks in your own games |
| 13 | [Defending Checkmate](LEVEL%2002%20-%20BEGINNER/13%20-%20Defending%20Checkmate) | • How to identify and stop an incoming checkmate threat <br> • Four main defensive tools to neutralize a mate threat under pressure <br> • How to choose the best defensive move rather than the first one you see <br> • When giving up material is better than getting checkmated <br> • How to recognize real defensive moves vs false defences |
| 14 | [Complete Your Opening](LEVEL%2002%20-%20BEGINNER/14%20-%20Complete%20Your%20Opening) | • How to fully finish your development in the opening phase <br> • How to activate all pieces—not just a few <br> • When the opening is considered “complete” <br> • Why moving the same piece too many times is a loss of tempo <br> • How to avoid the most common opening mistakes |
